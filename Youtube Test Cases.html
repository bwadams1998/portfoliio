YouTube Video Platform Test Cases

Test Case 11: YouTube Video Search

Test ID: YT-TC-001
Priority: High
Module: Search

What you need first:

  • Be on the YouTube homepage
  • Have an internet connection

Steps to test:

  1. Go to youtube.com
  2. Click on the search bar at the top
  3. Type in "guitar lessons"
  4. Press Enter or click the search icon

What should happen:

  • The search results page loads
  • You see videos related to guitar lessons
  • Each video shows a thumbnail, title, channel name, view count, and when it was uploaded
  • There are filters available (Upload date, Type, Duration, Features, Sort by)
  • Your search term appears in the search bar
  • You might see some related search suggestions

Test Data:

  • Search Query: "guitar lessons"

Test Case 12: Play Video

Test ID: YT-TC-002
Priority: High
Module: Video Player

What you need first:

  • Be on YouTube
  • Have search results or the homepage loaded

Steps to test:

  1. Click on any video thumbnail
  2. Watch what happens
  3. Check that the video controls show up

What should happen:

  • The video page loads
  • The video starts playing automatically (or waits for you to click, depending on your settings)
  • Video controls are visible (play/pause, volume, settings, fullscreen)
  • Below the video you can see the title, channel name, view count, and upload date
  • Recommended videos appear on the right side
  • The video loads smoothly without constant buffering (assuming decent internet)

Test Data:

  • Video: Any public video

Test Case 13: Subscribe to Channel

Test ID: YT-TC-003
Priority: Medium
Module: User Interaction

What you need first:

  • Be logged into your YouTube account
  • Be on a channel you haven't subscribed to yet

Steps to test:

  1. Go to any YouTube channel
  2. Find the "Subscribe" button (usually red, below the channel banner)
  3. Click "Subscribe"
  4. Watch the button change

What should happen:

  • The "Subscribe" button changes to "Subscribed"
  • The button color changes from red to gray
  • A bell icon appears next to it for notification settings
  • The channel's subscriber count goes up by one
  • The channel now appears in your "Subscriptions" feed
  • You might see a quick confirmation animation

Test Data:

  • Channel: Any public YouTube channel

Test Case 14: Like/Dislike Video

Test ID: YT-TC-004
Priority: Medium
Module: User Interaction

What you need first:

  • Be logged in
  • Have a video playing or loaded

Steps to test:

  1. Go to any video
  2. Find the like/dislike buttons below the video
  3. Click the "Like" button (thumbs up icon)
  4. See what happens to the button

What should happen:

  • The like button gets highlighted/filled in
  • The like count increases by one
  • You can click it again to unlike
  • If you had previously disliked it, that gets un-highlighted
  • The like gets saved to your "Liked videos" playlist
  • If you refresh the page, your like is still there

Test Data:

  • Video: Any public video

Test Case 15: Adjust Video Quality Settings

Test ID: YT-TC-005
Priority: Medium
Module: Video Player

What you need first:

  • Be watching a video
  • Have the video player visible

Steps to test:

  1. Start playing any video
  2. Click the settings icon (gear icon) in the player controls
  3. Select "Quality"
  4. Choose a different quality option (like 1080p, 720p, or 480p)
  5. Watch the video quality change

What should happen:

  • The settings menu opens
  • You see quality options (Auto, 2160p, 1440p, 1080p, 720p, 480p, 360p, etc.)
  • Your selected quality is marked or highlighted
  • The video adjusts to the new quality within a second or two
  • The video might buffer briefly while it switches
  • Your quality setting stays that way

Test Data:

  • Quality Change: From Auto to 1080p
